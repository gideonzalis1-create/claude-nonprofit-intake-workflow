# Sample Before/After Outputs

These examples use synthetic intake notes only. They are not real client records.

## Sample A: Workforce Support

### Before

Client recently lost part-time work after childcare schedule changed. Behind on phone bill, worried about missing job interview calls, wants short training program but cannot attend evenings. Has reliable bus access only on weekdays.

### Structured summary

**Confirmed facts**
- Client recently lost part-time work.
- Childcare schedule changed and affects availability.
- Client is behind on phone bill.
- Client is worried about missing job interview calls.
- Client wants a short training program.
- Client cannot attend evening programs.
- Client has reliable bus access only on weekdays.

**Possible resource categories to review**
- Employment / workforce support.
- Training or certification program navigation.
- Childcare or family support.
- Transportation support.
- Possible emergency phone or utility support if available locally.

**Missing information**
- Client's location and service area.
- Preferred work schedule.
- Existing childcare options.
- Type of training program desired.
- Whether the phone bill issue creates immediate risk of losing service.

### Follow-up questions

1. What kind of work are you looking for now?
2. What times are you available for work or training?
3. Do you currently have childcare coverage during the day?
4. Is your phone service at risk of being shut off?
5. What training fields are you most interested in?
6. Which bus routes or areas can you reliably access?

### Staff handoff note

Client appears to need workforce support connected to childcare and transportation constraints. They are seeking a short training program but cannot attend evenings. Staff should verify location, schedule, childcare situation, and whether phone service is at risk before routing. Possible categories to review include workforce training, childcare/family support, transportation, and phone-bill assistance if available. Do not promise placement or program eligibility.

## Sample B: Student Housing and Food Access

### Before

College student says financial aid refund is delayed, couch-surfing with friend, skipping meals, and unsure whether school has emergency help. Works weekends and speaks Spanish at home.

### Structured summary

**Confirmed facts**
- Client is a college student.
- Financial aid refund is delayed.
- Client is temporarily staying with a friend.
- Client is skipping meals.
- Client is unsure whether the school has emergency resources.
- Client works weekends.
- Client speaks Spanish at home.

**Possible resource categories to review**
- Housing stability.
- Food access.
- Student emergency aid.
- Benefits navigation.
- Language access, if client prefers Spanish-language materials.

**Missing information**
- Whether the client has a safe place to sleep tonight.
- Whether the school has an emergency aid or basic-needs office.
- Whether the client wants services in English or Spanish.
- Location and eligibility for local food resources.
- Whether the financial-aid delay has an expected resolution date.

### Staff handoff note

Student may need urgent basic-needs support related to temporary housing instability, food access, and delayed financial aid. Staff should first verify immediate safety and whether the client has a place to sleep tonight. Next steps may include checking school emergency aid, campus food pantry/basic-needs office, local food access, and language preference for follow-up materials. Do not imply eligibility for benefits or emergency aid until verified.

## Sample C: Healthcare Navigation

### Before

Parent missed clinic appointment because of transportation and language barriers. Needs help rescheduling and understanding paperwork. No medical advice requested.

### Structured summary

**Confirmed facts**
- Parent missed a clinic appointment.
- Transportation and language barriers contributed.
- Client needs help rescheduling.
- Client needs help understanding paperwork.
- No medical advice was requested.

**Possible resource categories to review**
- Healthcare navigation.
- Transportation support.
- Language access.

**Missing information**
- Preferred language.
- Clinic name and appointment type.
- Deadline or urgency.
- Whether interpreter services are available through the clinic.
- Transportation location and timing.

### Staff handoff note

Client needs healthcare navigation support related to rescheduling, paperwork understanding, transportation barriers, and language access. Staff should verify preferred language, appointment urgency, clinic interpreter options, and transportation availability. Claude should not provide medical advice. Any medical questions should be routed to qualified healthcare staff.
